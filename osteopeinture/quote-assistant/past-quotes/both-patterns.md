## 1. Common Scope Items & Line Item Patterns

| Exterior door jamb / soufflages | PEREIRA_01 |
| Interior windows (paired with exterior) | LANDO_02 (×3) |

### Description Language Patterns

- **French quotes** use terms like: *soufflages de portes et fenêtres*, *poutres et poteaux extérieures*, *apprêt à métal industriel*, *grattage, meulage, nettoyage*
- **English quotes** use: *scraping, chipping, caulking, sanding, spot priming, anti-rust treatment, abrasion of rust*
- Both languages consistently describe **prep work as included** within each line item (not a separate line)
- Prep language is standard across all quotes: cleaning, scraping loose paint, caulking, priming where needed
- **Optional/suggested items** are broken into a separate section (e.g., KAULBACH_02 "Optional / Suggested" for garage doors)
- **Extras and add-ons** are listed in a distinct sub-section when invoicing after the fact (LANDO_02 final invoice — Section B)

## 3. Paint Product Patterns — Exterior

| Surface | Product | Finish | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Exterior siding/wood/general | **Sherwin Williams Duration Exterior** | Eggshell or Satin | Used in KAULBACH_02, SMITH_01, LANDO_02, JEANNE-MANCE_01 |
| Metal railings / DTM surfaces | **SW DTM** (Direct-to-Metal) | Satin | LANDO_02 final invoice |
| Metal primer | **SW Kem Bond** (industrial metal primer) | — | JEANNE-MANCE_01 |
| Metal topcoat (alternative) | **SW B54** | — | LANDO_02 original quotes |
| Metal exterior beams | **SW Duration Exterior** | — | JEANNE-MANCE_01 |

### Key Observations

- **Sherwin Williams Duration Exterior** is the default exterior topcoat across nearly all projects — it is the clear house standard
- **Eggshell** is the most common finish for exterior wood (KAULBACH_02, SMITH_01); **Satin** appears in the finalized LANDO invoice
- Metal work uses a **two-product system**: industrial primer (Kem Bond or equivalent) + topcoat (SW DTM or SW B54 or Duration EXT)
- Colors are often **"color match existing"** for trim and windows; accent colors (e.g., SW 7069 Iron Ore, SW 7025 Backdrop) appear on feature elements like front doors and railings
- Paint costs are almost never broken out as a separate line — they appear to be **bundled into the per-item price**

## 4. Quantity Measurement & Description Methods

Exterior quotes at Ostéopeinture **do not use square footage or linear footage** as primary units. Instead:

| Method | Used For | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Unit count (per piece)** | Windows, doors, garage doors | "15 windows [450$ each]", "5 dormer windows [500$ each]" |
| **Lump sum per element** | Balconies, fascias, soffits, railings, beams | "$3,000 — Back Balcony", "$900 — Triangle Fascias" |
| **Hours + materials** | Repair add-ons | "8H + materials [$50]" |
| **No explicit quantity** | Soufflages, single doors, metal tables | Single-item descriptions, lump sum price |

> **No quote uses sqft or linear ft for exterior surfaces.** All pricing is either per-unit (windows, doors) or lump-sum per surface zone (deck, fascia, soffit, beams).

## 5. Duration & Day-Count Patterns

| Project | Duration |
|---|---|

| SMITH_01 | 3 days |

| LANDO_02 (original quotes) | ~7 days |
| PEREIRA_01 (mixed) | 3–4 days (visit 1) + 5–6 days (visit 2) |

### Observations

- Exterior-only projects: **3–7 days**, depending on scope
- Small single-element jobs (one door, one balcony): **3 days**
- Mid-size jobs (fascias + soffits + railings + balcony): **3 days**
- Specialized metal treatment (rust + multiple surfaces): **4–5 days**
- Large multi-element jobs (20 windows + doors + metalwork): **~7 days**
- Duration is given as a range or approximation ("approx 7 days"), never a hard commitment
- Multi-phase projects (PEREIRA_01) split into two separate visit blocks with start dates

## 7. Structural Differences: Exterior vs. Interior Quotes

| Dimension | Exterior Quotes | Interior Quotes |
|---|---|---|
| **Organization** | By surface element (windows, doors, railings, fascias) | By floor, then by room or surface type |
| **Floor references** | Absent or irrelevant | Central organizing principle |
| **Prep description** | Explicit per-item (scraping, rust treatment, caulking) | Often bundled as a general note |
| **Specialty prep** | Anti-rust treatment, meulage (grinding), industrial primer | PVA primer, plaster touch-ups |
| **Measurement unit** | Per unit (count) or lump sum per zone | Sometimes sqft for walls; also lump sum |
| **Paint products** | SW Duration EXT, SW DTM, SW Kem Bond, SW B54 | SW Duration Home, SW ProMar 200/400, SW Superpaint |
| **Finish** | Eggshell or Satin | Mat, Velours (Velvet), Perle (Pearl) |
| **Optional sections** | Yes — "Optional / Suggested" for items like garage doors | Rarely seen |
| **Extras section** | Yes — "B. Extras / Add-Ons" on invoicing | Not observed in interior quotes |
| **Taxes applied** | Sometimes included (LANDO, JEANNE-MANCE, PEREIRA), sometimes not (SMITH, KAULBACH) | Same mixed pattern |
| **Repair rate** | $60–$75/h + materials, stated as condition | Same |
| **Language** | Bilingual — French for structural/prep items, English for client-facing terms | Same pattern |

## 8. Formatting & Grouping Conventions

### Section Structure (Exterior)

1. **One section per surface type** — e.g., "Exterior Windows," "Doors," "Metal Work," "Fascias," "Soffits," "Balcony"
2. **Each section has a subtotal**, then a grand total at the bottom
3. **Optional items** are grouped in a dedicated "Optional / Suggested" section, clearly separated
4. **Extras/add-ons** on amended invoices are placed in a separate lettered section (e.g., "B. Extras / Add-Ons") after the original contract scope ("A. Initial Contract")
5. **No floor-based grouping** on exterior quotes — element type is the sole axis
6. **Interior windows paired with exterior** are included in the same exterior quote as a separate section (LANDO_02), likely because they were painted during the same exterior visit
7. Bilingual quotes separate French-language structural scopes (poutres, soufflages) from English client-facing descriptions

### Visual/Layout Conventions (inferred)

- Line items include **quantity × unit price** in brackets within the description: `"15 windows [450$ each]"`
- Repair extras use an **hours + materials formula**: `"8H + materials [$50]"`
- Paints are listed in a **separate paint schedule table** at the bottom of the quote, distinct from line items
